# Coffee Brewing Guide

## Ratios and water

For pour-over methods such as the V60, a good starting ratio is 1:15 — one
gram of coffee for every 15 grams of water. Water temperature should be
between 92°C and 96°C; water that is too hot extracts bitterness, while
water below 90°C tends to under-extract and taste sour.

Always use filtered water: coffee is more than 98% water, and high chlorine
or very soft water both ruin the cup.

## Espresso

Espresso is brewed by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee at
about 9 bars of pressure. A classic double shot uses 18 grams of ground
coffee to yield roughly 36 grams of liquid espresso in 25 to 30 seconds.
If the shot runs faster, grind finer; if it drips slowly, grind coarser.

## Cold brew

Cold brew uses a coarse grind and an immersion time of 12 to 24 hours at
room temperature or in the fridge, with a concentrate ratio around 1:5.
The result is naturally low in acidity and is usually diluted 1:1 with
water or milk before serving.

## Grind size and freshness

Grind size is the most important variable a home brewer controls: coarse
for French press, medium for drip, fine for espresso. Coffee is best
consumed within 4 weeks of the roast date, and should be ground right
before brewing — ground coffee loses most of its aromatics within 30
minutes.

## Storage

Store beans in an airtight, opaque container at room temperature. Never
store coffee in the fridge: it absorbs odors and the temperature swings
create condensation that degrades the beans.
